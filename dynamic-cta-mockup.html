Chiefs’ offense finds new rhythm as playoff picture tightens

By Jordan Ellis · Feb 3, 2026 · 4 min read

Chiefs offensive linemen run drills at Wednesday’s practice.

Kansas City’s offense looked sharper than it has in weeks on Sunday, with new personnel groupings opening up the middle of the field and giving Patrick Mahomes more time in the pocket than he’s had all season.

The shift comes as the Chiefs try to lock up a first-round bye, with three games left on the schedule and two divisional rivals close behind in the standings. Coaches say the changes were weeks in the making, not a reaction to one bad outing.

“It’s not about one game. We’ve been building toward this look since the bye week.”

The new-look line debuted against a defense that had generated pressure on nearly forty percent of opposing dropbacks over its previous three games, a number that fell sharply once Kansas City shifted its protection scheme in the second quarter.

Mahomes, for his part, downplayed any single adjustment, crediting the offensive staff for identifying tendencies on film and pointing to a full week of practice reps as the real difference-maker heading into Sunday.

With the postseason picture still fluid across the conference, every remaining possession carries extra weight — and Kansas City’s coaching staff appears determined to enter the playoffs with its identity, not just its record, settled.
