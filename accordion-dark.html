Why “Instant Summaries” Might Be the Best On-Page Feature in 2026

By Jordan Writer Feb 25, 2026 7 min read
Ad slot (GAM/AdSense placement) — stays put. Accordion starts collapsed.
  • Inline summaries help capture skimmers without forcing a modal.
  • Guided chips convert passive readers into active “askers.”
  • Escalate to a full overlay only after intent is shown.
  • Keep it collapsed on load to avoid pushing ads below the fold.
Mock only. In prod, this would open a chat overlay.

Most readers don’t actually read. They scan, scroll, bounce, and move on. That’s not a moral failing — it’s how the modern web is experienced. If you can deliver value in seconds, you can keep them longer.

Why an accordion works

A collapsed accordion doesn’t disrupt the page. When expanded by the user, it becomes an intentional action: “I want more.” That intent unlocks deeper interactivity, like guided chips and (eventually) a chatbot.

How to avoid layout drama

Start collapsed. Render fast. Load the heavy chat only after interaction. Keep ad slots stable. The result feels like editorial UX, not a bolted-on tool.

What you’d build next

In a real integration, those chips would map to specific question templates and return fast answers. After one or two chip taps, you’d transition to a full overlay chat with a clean exit back to the article.
